# ECON OVERHAUL v2 — WHOLE-FILE replacement of vanilla 01_orbital_deposits.txt.
# Each `produces` value is vanilla x (a PER-RESOURCE multiplier) — deposits made richer
# at the SOURCE so space is the primary BULK economy (replaces the removed flat +50%
# station modifier). Regenerated from vanilla by a tools-side script, NOT hand-edited.
#
# v2.5 (2026-06-26) per-resource multipliers (was a uniform x1.40 in v2.4, x1.75 in v2):
#   minerals  x1.40   energy x1.60   research (society/physics/eng) x1.15
#   alloys / food / consumer_goods / trade  x1.00 (VANILLA — not buffed)
# Rationale:
#   - minerals/energy = the Track-1 bulk flip; energy runs hotter (more sinks, drains faster),
#     minerals stay lower (they were the main GLUT culprit, open item #4 — x1.75 v2 -> x1.40).
#   - research only x1.15: Track 2 keeps research PLANET-primary; space research is a supplement.
#   - alloys left vanilla: alloys are a refined/STRATEGIC output — vision wants alloy scarcity
#     real, so the few rare orbital alloy deposits (d_alloys_1/2 spawn; 3+ are pc_junk-only) stay
#     un-inflated. food/consumer_goods left vanilla because they barely/never spawn (d_food_4..10
#     & the CG deposit are `always = no`; d_food_3 is ~0 weight) — buffing them is pure noise.
# ONLY produces values changed; drop_weight / habitat_modifier are verbatim vanilla.
# Re-sync from vanilla on any version bump. Tune the per-resource mults here. See compatibility.md.
